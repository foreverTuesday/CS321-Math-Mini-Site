More about Math at Simmons

Why Study math at Simmons?

When most people hear the name "Simmons College" they usually think of a small women's liberal arts college located in the heart of Boston. In terms of programs at the school, most people only know about the undergraduate nursing program or the graduate library sciences program. When most people think about those who choose to major in math they picture chalkboards in dusty classrooms, old men who lecture in a dull monotone, and complicated formulas that make no sense. So when someone who attends Simmons College mentions that they're majoring in math, people around them may express surprise that they're going to Simmons and studying math, that they're studying math and going to Simmons, or both. At first glance, the vibrant liberal arts student can seem so vastly different from the smart and serious math student that it can be hard to understand how they can be the same person.

Diverse Program Selection

Simmons has a wide variety of programs, including many that are not available at schools with better known math programs. Want to double major in math and women's studies, math and arts management, or math and social work? You'd be out of luck if you were studying at MIT instead of Simmons.

Diverse individuals

As a math major at Simmons, the vast majority of people you interact with on campus will be neither math students nor math professors. This means you will be friends with a much wider range of individuals than if you went to a school with a large math department.
